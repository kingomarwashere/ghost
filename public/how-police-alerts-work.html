How Ghost's Police Alerts Work

Fixed cameras never move, so a database handles those. Police patrols and mobile cameras do move — the only thing that can warn you about them is other drivers. Ghost turns every driver on the road into a live sensor. Here's how the crowdsourced layer works, end to end.

The loop: spot → report → confirm → alert

  1. Spot it. You pass a police car, a mobile camera, a crash or a hazard.
  2. Report it. One tap on the report button, pick the type. Your position is attached automatically — no typing while driving.
  3. Others confirm. The next drivers to reach that spot get the alert and can tap still there or gone.
  4. Everyone's warned. Anyone approaching within range gets a chime and an on-screen bar, in time to react.

Why confirmations matter

A report is only useful if it's current. Ghost keeps the map honest two ways:

  • Expiry — an active report ages out after about two hours on its own, so nothing lingers forever.
  • Confirm / deny — drivers passing the spot vote it up (still there) or down (gone). Real, present hazards get refreshed; things that have moved on get cleared quickly.

The more drivers using Ghost on a road, the sharper the picture — the network effect that made Waze work, pointed squarely at Australian conditions.

How you're warned while driving

  • Voice + chime — an audible alert so you never have to stare at the screen.
  • Coloured bar — red for police/hazards, amber for cameras, appearing as you close in.
  • Direction-aware — camera alerts respect which way the camera faces and which way you're travelling, cutting false alarms.
  • Hands-free by design — reporting is a single large tap; confirmations are one tap. Mount your phone and keep your eyes up.

Is it legal?

Ghost relays the same kind of information a passenger calling out "cop ahead" would give you, plus the locations of publicly signposted fixed cameras. You're responsible for driving to the road rules and for using your phone lawfully — mounted and hands-free. Ghost is a road-safety and awareness tool, not a licence to speed.

Frequently asked questions

How long does a police report stay on the map?

Active reports expire after about two hours. Confirmations keep a still-present report alive and denials clear stale ones, so the map reflects the road right now.

Do I need an account?

No. Reporting and receiving alerts need no sign-up — just open the map and allow location.

What types of things can I report?

Police, mobile cameras, crashes, hazards, road closures and traffic. Each has its own marker and approach alert.

Does it work in the background?

Ghost is a web app (PWA) you can install to your home screen. Keep it open while navigating for continuous alerts and voice guidance.
